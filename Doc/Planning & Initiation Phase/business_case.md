## 1. BUSINESS PROBLEM STATEMENT

### 1.1 Current Situation

**Context:**
- Sri Lanka has approximately **1.05 million hectares** of paddy cultivation (Dept. of Census & Statistics, 2024)
- Over **1.8 million farming families** depend on paddy farming for livelihood
- Paddy production contributes **1.7% to national GDP** (Rs. 190 billion annually)
- Average farm size: 0.5 - 1.5 hectares (small-scale farmers)

**Critical Problems Identified:**

#### Problem 1: Lack of Real-Time Crop Health Monitoring
**Current State:**
- Farmers rely on visual inspection (walking through fields)
- Health issues detected only when visible symptoms appear (often too late)
- No scientific measurement of vegetation health
- Large fields difficult to monitor consistently

**Impact:**
- Delayed intervention in pest/disease outbreaks
- 10-15% yield loss due to late problem detection
- Inefficient labor allocation for field monitoring

#### Problem 2: Inefficient Water & Fertilizer Management
**Current State:**
- Water application based on schedule, not actual plant needs
- Fertilizer applied uniformly across entire field
- No data on spatial variation in crop health
- Over-application in some areas, under-application in others

**Impact:**
- 30-40% water wastage in irrigated areas
- Rs. 5,000-10,000 wasted on excess fertilizer per hectare per season
- Environmental pollution from chemical runoff
- Reduced soil health over time

#### Problem 3: Unpredictable Yield & Poor Market Planning
**Current State:**
- No scientific yield prediction methods
- Farmers estimate harvest based on experience/guesswork
- Buyers/mills unable to plan procurement
- Price volatility due to supply uncertainty

**Impact:**
- Farmers sell at unfavorable prices (lack of negotiation power)
- Food supply chain inefficiencies
- Market speculation and price manipulation
- Rs. 10,000-20,000 potential income loss per farmer per season

#### Problem 4: Inadequate Disaster Impact Assessment
**Current State:**
- After floods/droughts, damage assessment is manual and subjective
- No scientific quantification of crop loss
- Delayed insurance claims (3-6 months typical)
- Disputes between farmers and insurance companies

**Impact:**
- Rs. 2-5 billion annual losses from natural disasters (uninsured)
- Farmer debt due to delayed compensation
- Discouraged insurance adoption (only 15% farmers insured)
- Mental stress and financial hardship

#### Problem 5: Information Asymmetry & Knowledge Gap
**Current State:**
- Farmers lack access to timely, localized agricultural information
- Extension officer ratio: 1:1000 farmers (insufficient coverage)
- Weather forecasts generic, not field-specific
- Agricultural advisories broadcast-style, not personalized

**Impact:**
- Poor decision-making leading to suboptimal outcomes
- Vulnerability to climate change
- Slow adoption of best practices
- Digital divide between urban and rural agriculture

### 1.2 Problem Quantification

| **Problem** | **Annual Economic Impact** | **Affected Farmers** |
|-------------|---------------------------|---------------------|
| Delayed health monitoring | Rs. 15-20 billion (15% yield loss) | 1.8M |
| Water/fertilizer inefficiency | Rs. 8-12 billion | 1.2M (irrigated areas) |
| Poor yield predictions | Rs. 5-8 billion (price volatility) | 1.8M |
| Disaster damage uncertainty | Rs. 2-5 billion (uninsured losses) | 500K (disaster-prone areas) |
| Information gap | Rs. 10-15 billion (opportunity cost) | 1.8M |
| **TOTAL ANNUAL IMPACT** | **Rs. 40-60 billion** | **1.8M farmers** |

### 1.3 Root Cause Analysis

**Why do these problems persist?**

1. **Technology Gap:** Lack of affordable, accessible precision agriculture tools
2. **Data Scarcity:** No systematic crop monitoring data collection
3. **Cost Barriers:** Commercial satellite imagery too expensive (Rs. 50,000-100,000 per image)
4. **Complexity:** Existing GIS tools require technical expertise
5. **Digital Literacy:** Limited smartphone/computer skills in rural areas
6. **Infrastructure:** Inconsistent internet connectivity
7. **Awareness:** Farmers unaware of satellite-based agriculture solutions

### 1.4 Consequences of Inaction

If these problems remain unaddressed:

**Short-term (1-2 years):**
- Continued 15-25% productivity gap vs. optimal yields
- Environmental degradation from resource mismanagement
- Farmer income stagnation
- Increased farmer debt (currently 60% farmers in debt)

**Medium-term (3-5 years):**
- Youth migration away from agriculture (sustainability crisis)
- Food security risks during climate events
- Increased food imports (Rs. 100+ billion rice imports potential)
- Loss of agricultural land to urbanization

**Long-term (5+ years):**
- Sri Lanka loses rice self-sufficiency status
- Rural economic collapse in farming communities
- Increased urban poverty from rural migration
- Environmental crisis (water scarcity, soil degradation)

### 1.5 Call to Action

**The problem is clear, quantified, and urgent.** A technology solution that democratizes access to satellite-based precision agriculture is not just beneficialâ€”it's essential for the sustainability of Sri Lankan paddy farming and national food security.

## 3. PROPOSED SOLUTION

### 3.1 Solution Overview

**SkyCrop** is a cloud-based, AI-powered agricultural intelligence platform that transforms satellite imagery into actionable farming insights. It consists of:

1. **Web Application** (Desktop/Tablet)
2. **Mobile Application** (Android/iOS)
3. **Admin Dashboard** (Content Management)
4. **AI/ML Engine** (Cloud-based processing)
5. **API Layer** (Third-party integrations)

### 3.2 Core Features & Capabilities

#### Feature 1: Intelligent Field Mapping
**What it does:**
- Farmer clicks on map to indicate approximate field location
- AI automatically detects field boundaries using satellite imagery
- Calculates accurate field area (hectares)
- Saves field boundaries for future monitoring

**Technology:**
- U-Net deep learning model for semantic segmentation
- Trained on DeepGlobe agriculture dataset
- 85-90% accuracy (IoU metric)

**Business Value:**
- Saves 30-60 minutes of manual boundary drawing
- 95%+ area calculation accuracy (vs. 70-80% manual estimation)
- Enables precise input calculations (seeds, fertilizer)

#### Feature 2: Crop Health Monitoring
**What it does:**
- Calculates NDVI (vegetation health), NDWI (water stress), TDVI (overall vigor)
- Color-coded field maps showing health zones
- Health status: Excellent / Good / Fair / Poor
- Historical trend graphs (last 6 months)

**Technology:**
- Sentinel-2 multispectral bands (Red, NIR, Green, SWIR)
- Cloud masking algorithms (ignore cloudy images)
- 5-day update frequency (Sentinel-2 revisit time)

**Business Value:**
- Early detection of stress (2-3 weeks before visible symptoms)
- Prevents 10-15% yield loss from delayed intervention
- Reduces crop scouting labor by 70%

#### Feature 3: Precision Agriculture Recommendations
**What it does:**
- Analyzes crop health indices
- Recommends specific actions:
  - Water: "Irrigate in 2 days" or "Adequate moisture, skip irrigation"
  - Fertilizer: "Apply 50kg/hectare urea to low-vigor zones"
  - Pest alert: "Check for signs of brown planthopper (low NDVI in Zone C)"

**Technology:**
- Rule-based expert system + machine learning
- Trained on agricultural best practices
- Personalized to field history and current conditions

**Business Value:**
- 20-30% reduction in water usage
- 15-20% reduction in fertilizer costs
- Rs. 8,000-13,000 savings per hectare per season

#### Feature 4: Yield Prediction
**What it does:**
- Predicts expected harvest quantity (kg/hectare)
- Updates prediction every 10 days throughout season
- Provides confidence interval (e.g., 4000-4500 kg/hectare)
- Alerts if yield trending below expectations

**Technology:**
- Random Forest regression model
- Features: NDVI time series, weather data, field area, historical yields
- 85-90% accuracy (MAPE < 10%)

**Business Value:**
- Better market planning and price negotiation
- Bank loan applications (proof of expected income)
- Early warning of poor yields â†’ corrective action
- Potential Rs. 10,000-15,000 additional income through better pricing

#### Feature 5: Disaster Impact Assessment
**What it does:**
- Compares satellite images before/after disaster event
- Calculates damaged area (hectares and %)
- Estimates financial loss (kg yield loss Ã— market price)
- Generates report for insurance claims

**Technology:**
- Change detection algorithms (NDVI differencing)
- CNN-based damage classification
- Spatial analysis (GIS operations)

**Business Value:**
- Objective damage quantification (reduces disputes)
- Faster insurance claims (weeks vs. months)
- Evidence-based disaster relief allocation
- Potential Rs. 30,000-100,000 faster claim processing per farmer

#### Feature 6: Weather Intelligence
**What it does:**
- 7-day weather forecast (temperature, rainfall, humidity)
- Field-specific alerts ("Heavy rain expected in 2 days - postpone fertilizer")
- Historical weather patterns
- Climate trend analysis

**Technology:**
- OpenWeatherMap API integration
- Location-based forecasts (10km resolution)
- Push notifications for critical weather

**Business Value:**
- Optimized timing of farm operations
- Reduced crop damage from weather events
- Better planning (spray schedule, harvest timing)

#### Feature 7: Knowledge Hub
**What it does:**
- Agricultural news and updates
- Farming best practices (articles, videos)
- Market prices and trends
- Government schemes and subsidies information

**Technology:**
- CMS (Content Management System)
- Admin dashboard for content creation
